## Key Takeaways

- The 'AI will take your job' narrative is deliberate — it suppresses wages by terrifying workers into accepting lower pay and avoiding salary negotiations.
- Companies publicly claim AI is transforming their productivity, but internally most have no idea how to make it actually work.
- Facebook's 'token leaderboard' forces employees to generate AI output volume at the expense of code quality — creating more work, not less.
- The 'Bitar Lesson': the more precision a task requires, the less useful AI becomes — LLMs approximate language, which approximates intent, which approximates output, creating an unbridgeable gap.
- AI reliably handles the easy 80% of work; the critical final 20% — quality, customer understanding, precision — remains entirely human.
- The AI hype cycle primarily benefits AI companies like Anthropic: every job cut or token mandate funnels corporate savings directly into AI spending.
- Workers lack a unified counter-narrative; the solution is radical honesty — sharing real stories about where AI is and isn't working.

## TLDR

The 'AI will steal your job' narrative is a deliberate wage-suppression strategy by tech elites — it spooks workers into accepting less while corporations redirect savings to AI vendors. Internally, most companies are faking AI success because the technology fundamentally can't handle precision work. The real power move is workers speaking openly about what AI actually does and doesn't do.
