## Key Takeaways

- Apple guided for 14-17% sales growth in the June quarter despite ongoing memory supply constraints, beating Wall Street expectations and sending shares up ~5% toward their best single day in over a year.
- iPhone sales grew 20% year-over-year, and China revenue hit $20.5 billion — well above the $18.9 billion analysts expected — signaling broad global demand across both developed and emerging markets.
- Apple's gross margin impact from the memory shortage was guided at only ~30 basis points, far less than the ~120 basis points the market had feared, demonstrating strong supply-chain management inherited from Tim Cook's era.
- Incoming CEO John Ternus hinted at a more flexible capital allocation strategy — potentially retaining more cash for deals and R&D rather than maximizing share buybacks, confirming the earlier CEO transition analysis that he's reshaping financial strategy, not just hardware.
- A macro analysis of 14 large AI-deploying corporations found that AI is boosting productivity but not displacing workers, and that return on invested capital has recently turned lower — raising questions about whether hyperscaler AI spending will pay off.
- OpenAI CFO Sarah Friar described a 'vertical wall of demand' for compute, pushing back on WSJ reports of missed internal revenue and user-growth targets.
- Twilio reported its fastest revenue growth in over three years, driven by AI-powered voice workloads — Voice channel growing 20% for six consecutive quarters of acceleration. AI voice emerging as a breakout use case.
- Roblox shares fell ~15% after aggressive age-verification and child-safety rollouts created short-term user-growth friction, connecting to the earlier Bloomberg video on Australia's social media ban and algorithmic liability.
- Peter Thiel's Founders Fund raised $6 billion — its largest fund ever — largely into AI companies like Anthropic and OpenAI, further concentrating capital in frontier AI.
- Employment for new college-graduate software engineers has fallen nearly 20% since 2022 as AI coding tools reduce entry-level hiring — validating Mo Bitar's wage suppression thesis and creating long-term talent pipeline risk for the industry.

## TLDR

Apple crushed expectations with 20% iPhone growth, $20.5B China revenue (vs $18.9B expected), and memory shortage margin impact at just 30bps vs 120bps feared. Incoming CEO John Ternus signalled a shift toward retaining more cash for M&A and R&D over buybacks. Entry-level software engineer hiring has dropped 20% since 2022 as AI coding tools reduce demand, while Twilio's AI-powered voice workloads emerged as a breakout use case with six consecutive quarters of acceleration.
